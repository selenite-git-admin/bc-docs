# UniBAT Reader Authoring Surface — Four-Layer Model, Per-Entity Observation Contract Binding & Chain-Resolvability Activation Gate

## Context

The Reader is repair-location A (source reality / admission boundary) and the SDG-blindness boundary — admission correctness enters the system here — but it is the only governed boundary with no authoring surface, so incorrect readers (wrong entities bound, no OC path, nondeterministic flavor) are creatable and activatable. The live AR reader proves the cost: active with 7 of 8 bound entities lacking any SO→CO path, and 0/65 flavors OC-linked. Per-entity OC binding (not one-per-flavor) is forced by the flavor reality — a flavor reads many source entities — and mirrors the already-correct per-entity reader_binding→AC pattern. The completeness + chain-resolvability gates are the closure rule (no admitted shape admissible until SC→AC→OC→CC resolves) applied to the Reader, the same discipline BCF/MCF already enforce. This closes the platform's last ungoverned boundary and is the critical-path entry the pilot AR feed depends on.

## Decision

ADR #R1 of the UniBAT Reader-boundary governance-parity sequence (from reader-boundary-governance-parity-study-2026-07-01.md Part 2). The Reader is the SDG-blindness boundary and the admission entry (repair-location A) — where admission correctness enters the system — yet it is the one boundary with NO governed authoring surface: thin CRUD (ReaderService.createReader, a name-uniqueness check) + createReaderFromOc (draft, one-OC-per-flavor) + bulk seed, versus BCF/MCF's authoring panels. The live AR reader is `active` with 7 of 8 bound source entities having no SO→CO path (binds BSID/BKPF/FAGLPOSE/I_BillingDocument/CustomerOpenItemSet/RVKRED_TS_POST_FLE/SGLPOS_C_CT — zero needed concepts — while the OCs describe BSAD/KURGV/TYPE_SD_S_MAP), and 0 of 65 flavors are OC-linked platform-wide.

Six decisions:

1. FOUR-LAYER MODEL as the governed reader shape: Reader (one per source_category/function/subfunction — the subfunction admission boundary) → Flavor (a source_system+scenario way to read it) → per-entity Binding, where every bound source entity carries BOTH an Admission Contract binding (exists: runtime.reader_binding) AND an Observation Contract binding (new). The Observation Contract MUST be bound per source entity, exactly like the Admission Contract.

2. PER-ENTITY OC BINDING [DBCP — approval-gated]. Introduce runtime.reader_observation_binding at per-entity granularity (reader, flavor, source_entity, environment) → observation_contract_id + version_code, with a version-safe surrogate PK + active partial-unique index (see Proposed DDL; corrected from a natural PK per audit F1). Retire the single reader_flavor.observation_contract_id field — a flavor MUST be able to observe many entities (the AR-family chain requires one flavor to read BSAD + KURGV + TYPE_SD_S_MAP together), yet one OC-pointer per flavor can express at most one entity's OC, which is precisely why SO→CO resolution (D336, gated on flavor.observationContractId) never fires. (Correction per audit F4: current live flavors each bind ≤1 active entity, so the single-OC field is not yet *visibly* broken; it becomes impossible the moment a flavor must observe the ≥2 entities every real metric chain needs. The claim is a capability requirement, not a current-state observation.) Physical drop of the old column deferred after confirming 0 populated (already 0 live).

3. SIX DESIGN POLICIES enforced by the authoring surface: (P1) subfunction uniqueness — at most one active reader per (source_category, function, subfunction); (P2) active-flavor determinism — at most one active flavor per (reader, source_system, scenario); environment is NOT part of flavor identity (it is resolved at the binding layer — see Change C), so the determinism key is environment-independent. Runtime selection matches the admission run's connection → (source_system, scenario) rather than flavors[0]. This keys determinism on the system axis without collapsing it: one AR reader legitimately has a SAP flavor, an Oracle flavor, a Tally flavor all active at once (that IS multi-system support) — the defect is ungoverned SELECTION, not flavor count; (P3) per-entity contract completeness — every bound entity must resolve SC→AC→OC→CC, a reader cannot activate with a bound entity lacking an OC; (P4) chain-resolvability activation gate (decision 4); (P5) versioning + draft→active lifecycle (reader_version is NULL today); (P6) environment/flavor binding consistency — one coherent binding set per (reader, environment).

4. CHAIN-RESOLVABILITY ACTIVATION GATE — the closure rule applied to admission: a reader activates only when its whole admission chain is resolvable per bound entity (same shape as MCF PE-MC-11 / the G3 source-chain gate). Fail-closed. The current AR reader (active with 7/8 entities unresolvable) is exactly the state this gate refuses.

5. GOVERNED READER-AUTHORING SERVICE, parallel to bcf-registry-authoring / the metric-authoring panel: validates the four-layer model against the 6 policies, drives the draft→active lifecycle with the completeness + resolvability gates, emits evidence — replacing the CRUD + createReaderFromOc + seed path.

6. GROUPING DOCTRINE: subfunction is the owning container (one reader per source_category/function/subfunction), with scenario/source-system/entity completeness beneath it. The reader taxonomy must cover the metric subfunctions it serves — the accounts-receivable-reader is the AR-family container for accounts_receivable + billing + credit_and_collections (they share source entities), a container-direction resolution of the reader↔metric subfunction mismatch.

PILOT APPLICATION (per P0, verified 2026-07-01): aligning AR through this surface = bind BSAD + KURGV + wire their per-entity OCs + pin the AR flavor + pass the resolvability gate → the 31 base AR metrics gain an SO→CO path. Two P0 nuances, corrected per the Codex audit (F2), scope the claim honestly:
- **Clearing-date / open-at-P family** (`ar_balance`, `ar_aging_*`, open-invoice-count) — semantically PROVEN: `as_of` with `closing_field=clearing_date` over BSAD computes open-at-P (an item now cleared was open at any P before its `clearing_date` — the SAP bitemporal pattern). BSAD supplies `BLDAT→document_date`, `AUGDT→clearing_date`, `WRBTR→gross_amount` directly.
- **Due-date-anchored family** (`overdue_*`, past-due, days-delinquent) — depends on a DERIVED canonical field, not a raw column: the active `cc__customer_invoice_arpi_slice` v8 derives `due_date = date_add(posting_date, net_payment_term_days)`. BSAD supplies the derivation INPUTS (`BUDAT→posting_date`, `ZBD1T→net_payment_term_days`); due_date is produced at the Canonical Contract. Note a latent redundancy to reconcile (out of #R1 scope): `oc__receivable_posting_bsad` ALSO maps `ZFBDT→"due date"` directly, but `ZFBDT` is SAP's *baseline* date, not the due date — the authoritative due_date is the CC derivation, and the raw ZFBDT→due_date mapping should be re-pointed to a baseline concept or dropped. Flagged for the AR OC-authoring pass.

Pilot first-target is a closed historical period where BSAD is complete; #R1 makes the entities observable/resolvable (an SO→CO path), it does not itself assert the full 31-metric E2E result — the multi-period `period_aggregate` caller-precondition and the metric temporal model remain metric ADR #2 (F8).

DB DELTA (Database Change Protocol — approval-gated; exact DDL delivered before any change): (A) new table runtime.reader_observation_binding (mirrors reader_binding, no FK across DBs where the pattern already omits them); (B) mark reader_flavor.observation_contract_id deprecated, physical drop deferred (0 populated). The authoring service + policy engine (3,4,5) are code-only, not DBCP.

Scope guard: this ADR is DESIGN-ONLY. No schema or code lands until explicit approval per the Database Change Protocol.

Sequence: ADR #R1 (this — authoring surface + four-layer model) · #R2 admission-run resumability/idempotency (pre-pilot) · #R3 reader evidence + version stamping · #R4 run-status honesty + failure taxonomy.

## System-agnosticism (the load-bearing property this model protects)

One AR Reader serves every source system — SAP, Oracle, Tally, Zoho Books — because system-specificity is confined to the Flavor and its bindings, never the Reader:

- **Reader** (`accounts-receivable-reader`) = system-agnostic subfunction admission boundary. Exactly one, regardless of how many systems the platform onboards.
- **Flavor** = one `(source_system, scenario)` way to read that subfunction: `sap-ecc-ar`, `oracle-fusion-ar`, `tally-ar`, `zoho-books-ar`. **One AR Reader has N flavors, all active concurrently** — this is multi-system support, not ambiguity.
- **Per-entity Binding under each flavor** = that system's entity → Admission Contract (admissibility of the raw shape) + Observation Contract (SO→CO: the system-specific field → the SAME canonical business concept). `SAP BSAD.WRBTR`, `Oracle AR_TRX.AMOUNT`, and `Tally Ledger.Amount` all map to canonical `gross_amount`.

Convergence happens at the **Canonical Object**: every flavor's OC lands the same canonical fields, so the CO — and everything above it (Canonical Contract, Metric Contract, Metric Snapshot) — is system-agnostic. A metric never names `BSAD` or a SAP field; it binds canonical concepts (the concept-identity join, `variable_role → business_concept → canonical field`). This is *why* per-entity OC binding is mandatory: multi-system = N flavors × per-entity OCs converging on one canonical vocabulary, and a single `observation_contract_id` per flavor cannot express even one system's multiple entities, let alone many systems.

**Corollary (this is why decision 3/P2 keys on the system axis):** flavor determinism is `(reader, source_system, scenario)` — environment-independent (environment lives at the binding layer, Change C), NOT `(reader, environment)`. Multiple active flavors under one reader is *correct*. The defect the gate fixes is ungoverned runtime *selection* (`flavors[0]`), which the runtime resolves by matching the admission run's connection → `(source_system, scenario)` to its flavor. The live AR reader's 5 active flavors (`apex-*` + `ecc-*`) also reveal a *modeling* smell the authoring surface corrects: flavor identity should be `(source_system, scenario)`, not a tenant name — tenant-specificity belongs to the connection/binding, not the flavor.

## Proposed DDL (decision 2 — Change A; DBCP-gated, not yet applied)

`runtime.reader_observation_binding` follows `runtime.reader_binding`'s per-entity granularity and soft-unbind pair, but with a **version-safe surrogate PK + active partial-unique index** (NOT a natural PK) — a deliberate divergence from `reader_binding`, corrected per the Codex audit (F1):

```sql
CREATE TABLE runtime.reader_observation_binding (
  binding_id              uuid PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),   -- surrogate; version-safe
  reader_id               uuid NOT NULL REFERENCES runtime.reader(reader_id) ON DELETE RESTRICT,
  flavor_id               uuid NOT NULL REFERENCES runtime.reader_flavor(flavor_id) ON DELETE RESTRICT,
  source_entity           text NOT NULL,
  environment_code        text NOT NULL DEFAULT 'dev',
  observation_contract_id uuid NOT NULL,      -- soft ref to contract.observation_contract (matches reader_binding's soft source_contract_id)
  version_code            text NOT NULL,
  bound_at                timestamptz NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  unbound_at              timestamptz          -- NULL = active (D162 Rule 9 temporal shape)
);
-- At most ONE ACTIVE OC binding per (reader, flavor, source_entity, environment).
-- History rows (unbound_at set) and rebinding to a NEW OC version are both allowed — the
-- partial predicate only constrains the active tuple. This is why a natural PK fails (F1):
-- a natural PK on (reader,flavor,entity,env) blocks ever pointing that tuple at a newer OC
-- version while the old row survives for history.
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX uq_reader_obs_binding__active
  ON runtime.reader_observation_binding (reader_id, flavor_id, source_entity, environment_code)
  WHERE unbound_at IS NULL;
CREATE INDEX idx_reader_obs_binding__oc ON runtime.reader_observation_binding(observation_contract_id);
```

Grain rationale: the OC binding's natural grain is `(reader, flavor, source_entity, environment)` — the OC is a semantic (SO→CO) contract selected per *flavor* (a source_system+scenario reads a given entity one way), whereas the AC is admissibility of the source shape. Rebinding an entity to a newer OC version = soft-unbind the active row (`unbound_at = now()`) + insert a new active row; the partial-unique guards single-active without blocking history or version rebind. This is deliberately **stronger than `reader_binding`**, whose natural PK includes `version_code` and which carries **no active-uniqueness guard** (two active AC versions for one entity are currently permitted) — so the ADR does not claim to "mirror `reader_binding` exactly"; it corrects the pattern (F5). `flavor_id` is NOT NULL here (an OC is always flavor-scoped — a SO→CO adapter is meaningless without a source system), even though it is nullable on `reader_binding`; the activation gate (below) specifies how reader-level (flavor-null) AC bindings pair with flavor-scoped OC bindings.

Change B: `reader_flavor.observation_contract_id` is marked deprecated in the schema (comment + removed from the write path); physical `DROP COLUMN` deferred to a follow-up migration after confirming 0 populated rows (already 0 live, 2026-07-01). No data migration — nothing to move.

Change C (F3 — scenario): promote `scenario` to an explicit dimension so P2's `(reader, source_system, scenario)` determinism is enforceable. Add `scenario_code text NOT NULL DEFAULT 'default'` to `runtime.reader_flavor` (today scenario is implicit in `flavor_name`/`config_json`, with only `source_system_name` explicit), tighten `source_system_name` to NOT NULL (source system is part of flavor identity; 0 null rows live 2026-07-01, so the tighten is data-safe — without it Postgres treats distinct NULLs as non-equal and the unique index below would permit multiple active null-system flavors, defeating P2, per audit item 3), and add a partial-unique for active-flavor determinism:
```sql
ALTER TABLE runtime.reader_flavor ADD COLUMN scenario_code text NOT NULL DEFAULT 'default';
ALTER TABLE runtime.reader_flavor ALTER COLUMN source_system_name SET NOT NULL;  -- data-safe: 0 nulls live
-- One ACTIVE flavor per (reader, source_system, scenario). Environment is resolved at the
-- binding layer (reader_binding/reader_observation_binding.environment_code), not on the
-- flavor — so flavor determinism is env-independent; the binding sets carry the env split.
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX uq_reader_flavor__active_scenario
  ON runtime.reader_flavor (reader_id, source_system_name, scenario_code)
  WHERE status_code = 'active' AND archived_at IS NULL;
```
Runtime flavor selection (a Part 1 / reader-runtime concern, not this table) then matches the admission run's connection → `(source_system, scenario)` to the one active flavor, replacing `flavors[0]`.

## Activation gate shape (decision 4 — code-only)

The gate shares the *shape* of MCF's PE-MC-11 / the G3 source-chain gate (fail-closed "closure rule" — no admitted shape admissible until the whole chain resolves) but is a **distinct, new reader-side evaluator** (`ReaderChainResolver`), not a reuse of the MCF evaluator — the MCF one resolves *metric variables* over `mcf.*`, this one resolves *reader bindings* over `runtime.*` + `contract.*` (corrected per audit F6: it is a coherent analogue, specified below, not a drop-in).

**Evaluation unit = `(reader, flavor, environment)`, not `(reader, environment)`.** Flavors are system-specific (a flavor is one `(source_system, scenario)`), and a source chain resolves *within* a flavor's world; evaluating across flavors would conflate SAP and Oracle entities. So: a **flavor** is activatable iff every `source_entity` it binds resolves; a **reader** is `active` iff it has ≥1 active flavor (it can be partially live — SAP flavor active, Oracle flavor still draft).

**Per-entity resolution:** for each `source_entity` a flavor binds, the resolver requires BOTH (a) an active `reader_binding` for that entity (flavor-scoped OR reader-level — a reader-level, flavor-null AC binding is paired to the flavor's OC binding by `(source_entity, environment)`, since AC is admissibility of the raw shape and is legitimately flavor-agnostic) whose `source_contract → admission_contract` version resolves, AND (b) an active `reader_observation_binding` whose `SC→AC→OC→CC` chain resolves.

**Break-reason taxonomy** (returned per `(flavor, source_entity)`, mirroring the L-node break-reason pattern, so the authoring surface names exactly what blocks activation):

| Reason code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `no_ac_binding` | entity has no active `reader_binding` (flavor-scoped or reader-level) |
| `ac_version_unresolvable` | AC binding present but its `source_contract`/`admission_contract` version does not resolve |
| `no_oc_binding` | entity has no active `reader_observation_binding` (the current AR defect — 7/8 entities) |
| `oc_inactive` | OC binding present but the OC/version is not active |
| `oc_cc_chain_unresolvable` | OC present but its `OC→CC` (canonical) chain does not resolve |

A flavor with any entity carrying a break reason stays `draft`; the reader cannot mark that flavor active. This is where policy 3 (completeness) and policy 4 (resolvability) are enforced in code — the exact refusal the live AR reader would have hit.

## Reader Flavor Topology Policy (2026-07-01, operator-locked — sharpens Policy 2 + the grouping doctrine)

The legacy AR flavors were created ad hoc (one per table, tenant-named). These are the GOVERNING RULES for every flavor, enforced in the authoring surface so future creations cannot repeat the mistake — not merely a one-time cleanup.

- **P-F1 — Identity = `(source_system_code, scenario_code)`.** A flavor is identified within a reader by that pair only — never by the table it reads or the tenant. Two active flavors of one reader may not share the pair.
- **P-F2 — `source_system_code` = source product + major data-model variant** (`sap_ecc`, `sap_s4hana`, `oracle_fusion`, `tally`, `zoho_books`). SAP ECC and S/4HANA are DIFFERENT systems (different tables — `BSAD`/`BSID` vs `ACDOCA`), hence different codes, not scenarios. Format: `snake_case`.
- **P-F3 — `scenario_code` = a finer within-system variant**, `default` otherwise. Never a table, tenant, or metric. `snake_case`.
- **P-F4 — One flavor reads MANY entities.** A flavor binds all the system's entities the subfunction needs, each via a per-entity Observation Contract (`reader_observation_binding`). One-flavor-per-table is PROHIBITED (the retired artifact).
- **P-F5 — One active flavor per `(reader, source_system, scenario)`.** Superseding = deactivate old + activate new. Enforced by `uq_reader_flavor__active_scenario` + the create/activate gates.
- **P-F6 — No tenancy in a flavor (or a binding environment).** A flavor describes a system's shape, shared across all tenants. Tenant identity lives ONLY in the **connection** (which tenant DB a binding points at). A binding's `environment_code` is the **deployment stage** (`dev`/`staging`/`prod`), NOT a tenant — the legacy AR bindings in an `apex` environment were exactly this leakage. Tenant-named flavors are PROHIBITED — `source_system_code` equal to any `tenant.tenants.slug` is rejected; tenant-named binding environments are non-conforming.
- **P-F7 — Naming: `{source_system_code}[-{scenario_code}]`** (kebab; e.g. `sap-ecc`, `sap-s4hana`). The name is DERIVED from the identity, not caller-supplied, so entity/tenant names are structurally impossible.
- **P-F8 — Legacy migrated through the authoring surface** (never raw-edited): create/keep one conforming flavor per real `(system, scenario)`, re-home entities as per-entity OC bindings, then deactivate + archive the non-conforming flavors (reversible `archived_at`).

**Enforcement (code):** `ReaderAuthoringService.createFlavor` derives the name from `(source_system_code, scenario_code)`, rejects non-`snake_case` codes (P-F2/P-F3), rejects a `source_system_code` that is a tenant slug (P-F6), and rejects a duplicate active-key flavor (P-F1/P-F5); flavors are created `draft` and reach `active` only through the resolver-gated `activateFlavor`. `archiveFlavor` performs the governed deactivate+archive (P-F8).

**AR application (S/4HANA archived-for-now, operator-locked):** consolidate the 11 AR flavors to ONE `sap-ecc` flavor binding `BSAD` + `KURGV` + `TYPE_SD_S_MAP` via per-entity OCs; archive the 7 `ecc-*` one-table flavors (entities re-homed), the 3 tenant-named `apex-*` flavors, and `s4hana-cloud-invoice-hdr` (a future `sap-s4hana` flavor, out of pilot scope).

## Independent audit refinements (Codex review, 2026-07-01) — all accepted
