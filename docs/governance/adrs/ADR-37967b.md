# Function/Subfunction Taxonomy — APQC PCF Alignment Adjustments

## Context

APQC PCF v7.3 cross-check revealed four gaps/misalignments in BareCount master taxonomy before populating 1,800+ KPIs. Fixing these before batch decomposition prevents cascading re-work. APQC is the industry-standard process taxonomy used by Oracle, SAP, and most enterprise BPM tools — alignment ensures BareCount KPI codes are interoperable and defensible.

## Decision

Four adjustments to master function/subfunction taxonomy, validated against APQC Process Classification Framework v7.3:

1. PAYROLL STAYS UNDER FINANCE — APQC places payroll under Human Capital (7009). BareCount keeps it under Finance as an operational choice. Documented as intentional deviation. Cross-function KPI contracts referencing payroll data must note this placement.

2. REVENUE ACCOUNTING ADDED TO FINANCE — New subfunction covering revenue recognition, deferred revenue, contract assets/liabilities, ASC 606 / IFRS 15 compliance. Maps to APQC 10002. Distinct KPI profile from AR (which covers cash collection) and GL (which covers period-end reporting).

3. COMPENSATION & BENEFITS SPLIT INTO TWO — 
   - Compensation: salary benchmarking, pay equity, total compensation ratios, merit distribution. Maps to APQC 7004 (Reward and Retain Employees).
   - Benefits Administration: enrollment rates, cost per employee, benefits utilization, plan participation. Maps to APQC 7007 (Manage and Administer Benefits).
   These have materially different KPI profiles and should not share a subfunction.

4. EMPLOYEE INFORMATION & ANALYTICS ADDED TO HR — New subfunction covering HR data quality, headcount accuracy, people analytics, HRIS administration, workforce reporting. Maps to APQC 7006. Increasingly strategic as HR moves toward data-driven decisions.

Result: Finance has 13 subfunctions (was 12, added Revenue Accounting). HR has 9 subfunctions (was 8, split Comp&Ben into two, added Employee Analytics).
