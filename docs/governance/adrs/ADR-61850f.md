# D441 Foundation-shape lock — BC semantic_role + canonical_value_set storage

## Context

See decision text below.

## Context

DEC-6b35e0 (D441) — *Source vocabulary discipline at the MC boundary* — decided 2026-06-13 at bc-docs-v3 main commit `2e56be2`. The implementation train begins with this shape-lock decision (Packet 0 in the planning packet `bc-core/scripts/audit-output/d441-implementation-train-planning-packet-2026-06-13.md`).

Two new BC-side concepts are introduced by D441:

1. `semantic_role` — classifies whether a BC may be used as a strategic filter target. D441 rules 3 and 5 (PE-MC-12 diagnostic-BC refusal) depend on this classification.
2. `canonical_value_set` — declares the canonical literals a BC accepts. D441 rules 2 and 5 (PE-MC-12 literal-membership refusal) depend on this.

Both must land in substrate before PE-MC-12 (Packet 4) and the Maker prompt update (Packet 5) can reference them. Their exact shape determines whether Packets 1 and 2 ship independently and whether existing BC supersession mechanics remain stable.

This is small but load-bearing. Getting the shape wrong forces re-migration of BC rows.

Operator confirmed (2026-06-13) two specific shape-lock concerns:
- canonical_value_set ownership belongs on the BC, not the CC field_selection entry (CC may narrow but never invent).
- semantic_role must be versioned, because reclassifying document_type_code from usable to diagnostic is governance-significant.

## Decision

### D1 — `semantic_role` enum values

Eight values, snake_case, ISO-11179 compatible:

| Value | Meaning | Examples among existing BCs |
|---|---|---|
| `strategic_filter` | BC may be bound as a target in MC `filter_clauses` | future receivable_event_kind (Packet 6a) |
| `diagnostic` | BC is observable for audit but NOT usable as filter target | document_type_code post-reclassification (`f10afed1`) |
| `identity` | BC is a key/identifier on its grain | document_number, customer_id |
| `temporal` | BC carries a date/time value | posting_date, clearing_date, document_date, ship_date |
| `amount` | BC carries a monetary or quantitative numerical value | payment_amount, gross_amount, net_amount, ordered_quantity |
| `status` | BC carries lifecycle state (a specialization of strategic_filter, kept distinct for diagnostics) | Customer Invoice status, Customer Payment status (`5c105543`), Customer Shipment status |
| `dimension` | BC is a slicing/grouping attribute | currency_code, country_code, industry_code |
| `reference` | BC points to another grain entity | exchange_rate, payment_terms |

`status` overlaps `strategic_filter` conceptually but is kept distinct. The role records the BC's PRIMARY semantic purpose; a status BC remains filter-bound as long as the literal is in its canonical_value_set (D441 rule 2). Future enum additions require a sibling decision, not a casual amendment.

### D2 — `semantic_role` is versioned

`semantic_role` is stored per-version, on `concept_registry.business_concept_version`, not on the lineage anchor `concept_registry.business_concept`.

Rationale:
- Reclassifying document_type_code from (untyped today) to `diagnostic` (Packet 6a) is a governance act. It MUST produce a new BC version + supersession record + cert. Lineage-level role would skip that chain.
- M14-active MCVs bind to a specific `business_concept_version_id` at activation. PE-MC-12 reads role from THAT version, not from the lineage's current `is_current` version. This is the foundation of Trap D mitigation in the planning packet: existing M14-active MCs see the role active at their binding time, not the reclassified role today.
- Lineage-level role would require ALTER ON the immutable BC lineage table; the substrate trigger refuses.

Schema:
- `concept_registry.business_concept_version` ADD COLUMN `semantic_role text NOT NULL` after backfill (nullable during migration window).
- CHECK constraint enumerates the 8 D1 values.
- `concept_registry.business_concept` (lineage anchor) carries NO `semantic_role` column.

Backfill: every active BC version receives a semantic_role via governed batch tool (never raw UPDATE). Ambiguous BCs (e.g. `document_number` between identity and diagnostic) are operator-classified in batch review.

### D3 — `canonical_value_set` ownership

**The Business Concept owns the canonical vocabulary. CC field_selection may narrow (subset) the BC's set per-CC, but may NOT invent new values.**

Rationale (per operator §4 addition):
- A canonical concept's value vocabulary is intrinsic to the concept's meaning — not to a particular CC's slice.
- Allowing CC to invent new values would let one CC introduce vocabulary another doesn't recognize, fragmenting canonical semantics across CCs.
- Narrowing is permitted: a CC slice (e.g. `cc__customer_payment_arpi_slice`) may declare it accepts only status ∈ {cleared, in-clearing}, a strict subset of the BC's full lifecycle vocabulary.

Schema:
- `concept_registry.business_concept_version` ADD COLUMN `canonical_value_set jsonb NULL`. NULL means "no enumerated set at BC level; this BC carries free-form values" (e.g. document_number, document_date).
- When non-null: structure `["value1","value2",...]` (string array).
- `contract.canonical_contract.body.field_selection[].canonical_value_set` is the OPTIONAL CC narrowing override. When non-null, the CC validator verifies it is a strict subset of the bound BC's set, or refuses with `cc_field_selection_invented_value`.
- BC value-set widening (adding values) is a BC supersession; CC narrowing of an existing BC set is a CC supersession.

### D4 — `canonical_value_set` is versioned

Same model as D2: stored on `business_concept_version`, captured at MCV-binding time, immutable after that. BC supersession that widens or narrows the set produces a new BC version.

### D5 — Read-time resolution algorithm

When PE-MC-12 (Packet 4) or any consumer asks "what is the canonical_value_set for this MCV's filter_clause?":
1. Read the MCV's bound `business_concept_version_id` from the filter clause row.
2. Read `canonical_value_set` from that BC version.
3. If a CC narrowing applies (the CC field_selection version through which the MCV resolved declared a narrower set), use the CC's narrower set; otherwise use the BC's set.
4. Test literal membership against the resolved set.

This makes PE-MC-12 deterministic per MCV and stable across BC future supersessions — a refactor of the receivable_event_kind BC value set in 2027 does not retroactively invalidate a 2026 MCV that bound the BC's 2026 version.

### D6 — `canonical_value_set` requirement matrix by `semantic_role`

The value set is REQUIRED for roles whose semantic purpose is discrete-vocabulary filtering. It is NOT applicable for continuous-domain or free-form roles. The check is enforced at BC admission time, not deferred to PE-MC-12 runtime.

Added per operator amendment 2026-06-13: without this, a future authoring session could mint `receivable_event_kind` with `semantic_role='strategic_filter'` but a NULL `canonical_value_set`, defeating the purpose of D441 by leaving the door open to ad-hoc literals.

| `semantic_role` | `canonical_value_set` | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| `strategic_filter` | **REQUIRED** (non-empty array) | The role exists to enable canonical-literal filtering; without the set the pattern collapses to ad-hoc |
| `status` | **REQUIRED** (non-empty array) | Lifecycle vocabularies are by definition enumerated; ad-hoc status literals defeat the canonical layer |
| `dimension` | **OPTIONAL** | If discrete (e.g. ISO 4217 currency codes) declare it; if free-form (e.g. industry classifications across standards) leave NULL. Operator decides at mint time |
| `diagnostic` | **N/A** | Filter prohibition (D3) makes the value set moot |
| `identity` | **N/A** | Identifiers are free-form by nature (document numbers, UUIDs) |
| `temporal` | **N/A** | Dates/timestamps are continuous-domain values |
| `amount` | **N/A** | Monetary/numerical values are continuous-domain |
| `reference` | **N/A** | References are by-key, not by-value |

#### D6.1 — BC admission-time enforcement (B6 createConcept + F3 supersedeBusinessConcept)

When admitting a BC version with `semantic_role IN ('strategic_filter','status')`, the validator MUST verify `canonical_value_set IS NOT NULL AND array_length(canonical_value_set) >= 1`. Refusal verdict: `bc_strategic_role_missing_canonical_value_set`. The failure surfaces at BC authoring — loud and immediate — not at downstream filter-use time. Packet 1 (TSK-fadcb0) ships this admission validator in the same change as the `semantic_role` column, so Packet 6a (TSK-b35b45) cannot accidentally create the gap.

#### D6.2 — PE-MC-12 runtime behavior with NULL `canonical_value_set` (defense in depth)

Combined with D5's resolution algorithm:

| Operator | `semantic_role` (at bound BC version) | `canonical_value_set` (resolved per D5) | Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| `is_null` / `is_not_null` | any | any | **PASS** (structural predicate, vocabulary-agnostic) |
| equality / membership | `diagnostic` | any | **REJECT** `mc_filter_uses_diagnostic_bc` (existing D441 rule 5) |
| equality / membership | `strategic_filter` or `status` | NULL | **REJECT** `mc_filter_strategic_role_missing_canonical_value_set` (defense in depth — should have been caught at D6.1, but Packet 4 still checks) |
| equality / membership | `strategic_filter` or `status` | non-NULL, literal NOT IN set | **REJECT** `mc_filter_uses_source_vocabulary` (existing D441 rule 5) |
| equality / membership | `dimension` | non-NULL, literal NOT IN set | **REJECT** `mc_filter_uses_source_vocabulary` |
| equality / membership | `dimension` | NULL | **PASS** (free-form dimension permitted; operator chose not to enumerate at mint time) |
| equality / membership | `identity`, `temporal`, `amount`, `reference` | N/A | **PASS** (continuous/free-form domains by nature) |

#### D6.3 — CC narrowing constraint by role

The CC narrowing override (D3) is meaningful only when the bound BC has a discrete vocabulary to narrow. The CC validator MUST refuse a `canonical_value_set` narrowing override when the bound BC version's `semantic_role IN ('identity','temporal','amount','reference')` — those roles have no enumerable set to narrow. Refusal verdict: `cc_field_selection_narrowing_unsupported_role`. Narrowing remains valid for `strategic_filter`, `status`, and (if BC declared a set) `dimension`.

## Consequences

**Accepted:**
- Two new columns on `business_concept_version` (semantic_role text, canonical_value_set jsonb). Both per-version.
- Per-version storage means BC supersession is the act that changes role or value set — never a hand-edit.
- CC supersession is the act that changes CC narrowing.
- Cross-version reads required: M13/PE-MC-12/Maker prompt all read the BC version captured at binding, not the lineage anchor's current `is_current` version.

**Avoided:**
- Re-migration of BC rows if operator later wants to evolve role/value-set semantics — per-version storage absorbs the change.
- BC immutability trigger conflict — lineage table untouched.
- Vocabulary fragmentation at CC layer (D3).
- Retroactive invalidation of M14-active MCVs when BC versions evolve (D5).

**Open follow-up captured in train TSK list:**
- TSK-fadcb0 (Packet 1): apply DDL for `semantic_role` on `business_concept_version` + backfill plan + B6/F3 surface to accept role on new BCs + **D6.1 admission validator** (`bc_strategic_role_missing_canonical_value_set` refusal). The admission validator MUST land with the column, not in a separate later packet — otherwise Packet 6a could exploit the gap.
- TSK-7ed42a (Packet 2): apply DDL for `canonical_value_set` on `business_concept_version` + CC narrowing validator (the `cc_field_selection_invented_value` refusal from D3) + **D6.3 narrowing-by-role validator** (`cc_field_selection_narrowing_unsupported_role` refusal) + backfill plan for BCs with definition-declared sets (e.g. Customer Payment status `5c105543`).
- TSK-879332 (Packet 4): PE-MC-12 reads role and set per the D5 resolution algorithm and applies the **D6.2 NULL-handling matrix** (defense in depth).
- TSK-b35b45 (Packet 6a): mints `receivable_event_kind` with `semantic_role='strategic_filter'` AND `canonical_value_set=['customer_invoice','customer_payment','credit_memo','debit_memo']` in a single atomic admission — the D6.1 validator from Packet 1 enforces this by refusing the mint otherwise.

## Rejected alternatives

- **Storage on lineage anchor `business_concept`** — requires ALTER on immutable BC table; substrate trigger refuses. Also loses the per-MCV-binding stability of D5.
- **Storage inside BC body (JSONB)** — couples role/value set to the immutable body shape; reclassification requires body rewrite which the immutability trigger refuses.
- **`canonical_value_set` on CC only** — fragments vocabulary across CCs; operator §4 rejects this.
- **`canonical_value_set` allows CC to introduce new values** — same fragmentation risk; operator §4 rejects.
- **Lineage-level `semantic_role` with audit log** — works but loses D5's per-binding stability. Bigger blast radius if reclassification is wrong.
