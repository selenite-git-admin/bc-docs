# Spine expansion: add AI and Development sections; split Services into three chapters; add Notifications and Webhooks and Audit Logging chapters

## Context

Two distinct concerns drove the expansion. (1) Founder review of the seven-chapter Implementation section flagged that "Architecture and Services" was too dense for one chapter (service inventory, ports, two-database topology, auth boundary, AWS profile, CodeArtifact, Docker, pm2 rationale — 6-8 chapters' worth of content). When the discussion opened up the question of how many services the platform actually has, the count was ~8 deployable services + ~30+ NestJS modules inside bc-core + several AI sub-services + ~5 external dependencies. A single chapter cannot justify that depth. The split into Architecture (topology), Backend Services (deployables), Internal Modules (NestJS modules within bc-core), and Auxiliary Services (smaller deployables) preserves a single-chapter granularity while honoring the depth. (2) AI is a horizontal concern that doesn't fit cleanly into one section: it touches Operating Model (gates, agents, AI Contract grammar provisional), Implementation (bc-ai service), Development (Claude Code itself as our tooling), and Compliance (hallucination handling, audit). It also evolves on its own velocity ("always evolving" per founder) and has its own audiences (auditor wants AI footprint clear; tenant wants AI transparency). A dedicated AI section centralizes that story; cross-cutting touchpoints continue to live in their canonical sections. (3) DevHub-as-name-implies is about development not operations (founder); the prior "DevHub and Governance" chapter that lived in Implementation was a category error since DevHub is the tool we use to build the platform, not part of the built artifact. (4) Build and Release, Quality Assurance (bc-qa), Documentation System, Developer Experience are all dev-time concerns that don't fit Implementation (they aren't part of the platform) or Operations (they aren't about running the platform). A dedicated Development section is the honest home. (5) Two missing platform-feature chapters surfaced during scope review: Notifications and Webhooks (email outbound, webhook receivers, integration surfaces) and Audit and Activity Logging (operational audit logs distinct from Evidence/Lineage proof chain). These are governed platform features without homes in the prior structure. Refines DEC-3b86ea (Onboarding/Operations rename and chapter cleanup), DEC-376587 (Implementation rename), and DEC-9c58c6 (article drop). The seven-chapter Implementation section was a working approximation; this ADR is the considered final structure after deep scope review. Counts: 79 chapters total. Eight sections is one above the original six but each section is honestly distinct in role; the asymmetry is structural (some sections are spec, some are artifact, some are process), not register-asymmetric.

## Decision

The bc-docs-v3 spine expands from six sections to eight. Two new sections are added between Implementation and Onboarding: (1) AI — a dedicated section for the platform's AI architecture, services, agents, gates, trust model, and tenant-facing visibility; (2) Development — a dedicated section for the engineering process and tooling that builds and evolves the platform (DevHub, decision and change workflow, build and release, quality assurance, documentation system, developer experience). Implementation is reshaped: the prior single "Architecture and Services" chapter splits into Architecture, Backend Services, Internal Modules, and Auxiliary Services (four chapters); two new platform-feature chapters are added (Notifications and Webhooks; Audit and Activity Logging); AI Architecture and DevHub and Governance move out of Implementation entirely (to AI and Development sections respectively). The new spine reads: Foundation, Operating Model, Implementation, AI, Development, Onboarding, Operations, Compliance. Per-section chapter counts: Foundation 9 (incl. overview) + Operating Model 17 (incl. overview) + Implementation 11 + AI 7 + Development 7 + Onboarding 15 + Operations 8 + Compliance 5 = 79 chapters total.
